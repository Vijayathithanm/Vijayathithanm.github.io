Profile

Where surface science meets manufacturing intelligence

I design and engineer high-performance thin-film coatings and cutting tools and the simulation and data pipelines that prove they work. My day-to-day spans HiPIMS/PVD coating development, indigenous magnetron design via COMSOL multiphysics, and finite-element analysis of machining and tribological damage.

At Addlife Coatings, I develop advanced HiPIMS recipes that extend cutting-tool life, design round tools (drills, mills, reamers) for manufacturability in SolidWorks, and validate performance through tribology, SEM, and adhesion testing. My PhD at IIT Madras focused on the advanced machining of carbon-fiber composites — damage mechanisms, tool wear, and predictive modelling using acoustic-emission sensing and machine learning.

I bridge the lab and the shop floor: from plasma physics and material characterization to predictive maintenance, digital twins, and ML-driven process optimization.
